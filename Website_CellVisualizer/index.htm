The CellVisualizer Software Project

CellVisualizer software is designed for complex and interactive exploration of multidimensional data, specifically from high-throughput image-based experiments. Measurements can be explored on an individual-cell basis, or grouped by the image in which the cells were contained, or grouped by the sample conditions from which the image came. Interesting samples can further be explored by linking to the original image data or to web-based information about the samples (e.g. gene information).

We are currently developing CellVisualizer to explore the huge datasets produced by CellProfiler open-source cell image analysis software. A typical experiment contains ~500 image-based measurements for each of ~10 million cells. The project team is based at the Broad Institute Imaging Platform, and consists of Anne E. Carpenter, Thouis Ray Jones, InHan Kang (the primary developer), and Adam Papallo. The project was founded in the laboratories of David M. Sabatini and Polina Golland at the Whitehead Institute for Biomedical Research and MIT's CSAIL.

This software is under extensive development and is not yet ready for release. For more information, contact Anne.
